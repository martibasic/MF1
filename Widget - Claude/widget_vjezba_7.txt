Zadatak 1 – Rezultantna sila na cilindar (half-submerged, fluid on left)
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget for hydrostatic forces on a full cylinder of radius R, where fluid fills the left half and the cylinder sits against a wall, with atmospheric pressure on the right side and below. Add sliders for: fluid depth above cylinder center h (0.5–4 m), radius R (0.3–1.5 m), cylinder length b (0.5–3 m), fluid density ρ (800–1200 kg/m³). Draw the cross-section with labeled points A (bottom), B (left), C (center), D (top), E (right), and reference points F, G, H on the free surface. Color the fluid region blue. Show the volume decomposition for the vertical force component using two differently colored hatched regions: (1) blue-hatched region = fluid volume above the LEFT half of the cylinder (pushes down), (2) red-hatched region = imaginary fluid volume above the RIGHT half of the cylinder (negative contribution, pushes up). Animate F_H as a horizontal arrow (force on vertical projection 2R×b, acting at depth h + R from surface). Animate F_V as a vertical arrow = net weight of the two hatched volumes. Show the resultant F_R as a diagonal arrow from the cylinder center. Display the angle α = atan(F_V/F_H). Add a toggle button to show/hide each volume contribution separately. Update all force arrows and numerical values live when sliders change.


Zadatak 2 – Rezultantna sila na polu-cilindrični zatvarač (60° arc)
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget for hydrostatic forces on a semi-cylindrical gate (half-cylinder) oriented with fluid above, where the gate spans a 60° arc from vertical, submerged at depth H below the free surface. Add sliders for: total water depth H (1–7 m), cylinder radius R (0.5–2 m), gate width B (1–4 m), arc angle α (30°–90°), and fluid density ρ. Draw the cross-section showing the curved gate as a half-circle arc with labeled points: A (top of arc), B and C (mid-level), D and E (bottom), F (bottom center), and G, H, I on the free surface. Show the fluid region above and around the gate. Decompose the vertical force using two colored hatched regions: V1 (rectangular block above the arc, blue hatching) and V2 (semicircular segment, red hatching, subtracted). Animate F_H as a horizontal arrow acting on the vertical projection (height = 2R·sinα, width B) at its center of pressure. Animate F_V (downward) = ρ·g·(V1 - V2)·B. Show the resultant F_R arrow from the gate center with its angle. Label dimensions 2R·sinα (vertical extent) and 2R·cosα (horizontal extent) on the diagram. Add a "Play" button that slowly increases water level H showing all forces growing proportionally. Display all numerical values live.

Widget A – Interaktivni explorer: Horizontalna vs. Vertikalna komponenta na kružnom luku
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget that lets students explore how horizontal and vertical hydrostatic force components arise on any circular arc submerged in fluid. Draw a circle of radius R centered at origin. Add a draggable arc selector: the user can drag two points on the circle to define the start and end angle of the submerged arc (from 0° to 360°). As the arc changes, animate: (1) the vertical projection (shadow) of the arc onto a vertical wall on the left, colored red — F_H acts on this projection, (2) the horizontal projection (shadow) onto a horizontal floor, colored blue — F_V equals weight of fluid column above this projection. Show hatched fluid volumes above and below the arc with different colors, updating in real time as the arc moves. Display F_H and F_V as animated arrows whose lengths scale with magnitude. Show the resultant F_R arrow and its angle α. Add sliders for fluid depth to arc top (0–5 m), R (0.5–2 m), density ρ. Include a physics insight panel explaining: "F_H always equals force on the FLAT vertical projection" and "F_V always equals weight of fluid ABOVE the curved surface." Animate smoothly as arc angles change.


Widget B – Tlačna kutija: vizualizacija hidrostatskog tlaka po površini zakrivljene ploče
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget that visualizes the pressure field acting normal to a curved surface at every point along the arc. Draw a quarter-circle arc (concave side facing up-left, fluid above). For every point along the arc, draw a small pressure arrow pointing normal (perpendicular) to the surface, with length proportional to the local hydrostatic pressure p = ρ·g·z at that depth z. Animate the arrows: as the user moves a "water level" slider, all arrows grow simultaneously, deeper ones always longer. Color the arrows on a gradient from light blue (low pressure, near surface) to dark blue (high pressure, deep). Show that the horizontal components of all arrows sum to F_H, and vertical components sum to F_V — animate this summation visually by projecting each arrow onto x and z axes. Add a button "Show projections" that draws faint horizontal and vertical dashed lines from each arrow tip to the axes, building up the total force bars on the side. Include sliders for R, h (submersion depth), arc angle range, and ρ. Update everything live.


Widget C – Usporedba: ravna vs. zakrivljena površina iste projekcije
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget that places two surfaces side by side: LEFT = a flat vertical rectangular plate, RIGHT = a curved surface (quarter circle arc) with the same vertical and horizontal projected dimensions. Both are submerged to the same depth with the same fluid. Show pressure distribution diagrams on both: for the flat plate, a triangular/trapezoidal pressure diagram on the side. For the curved surface, normal pressure arrows at multiple points along the arc. Animate arrows showing that F_H is identical for both (same vertical projection) but F_V is zero for the flat plate and nonzero for the curved surface. Add sliders for water depth, plate/arc height, width b, and density ρ. Show a live comparison table: F_H (flat) vs F_H (curved), F_V (flat=0) vs F_V (curved), F_R (flat) vs F_R (curved), angle α. Highlight the key insight: curved surfaces have an extra vertical component due to the fluid weight above them. Color code: flat plate in orange, curved surface in blue.


Widget D – 3D intuicija: cilindar u fluidu, sile po kutovima
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget showing a cylinder cross-section (circle) fully or partially submerged, where the user can rotate the cylinder's submersion angle interactively. Draw the circle with a slider "submersion angle" from 0° (just touching surface) to 360° (fully submerged). As the angle increases, the fluid level rises around the cylinder and the submerged arc grows. For each submersion state, animate: (1) F_H arrow (horizontal) growing as more vertical projected area is submerged, (2) F_V arrow (vertical) growing as more fluid volume sits above the arc, (3) net resultant F_R rotating as the ratio F_V/F_H changes. Draw a live polar plot in the corner showing F_R magnitude vs submersion angle as a curve — students can see how the resultant rotates and grows nonlinearly. Add a "special cases" button panel: click "Half submerged left", "Quarter arc top", "Full cylinder" — each snaps to that configuration and highlights what cancels and what remains. Include ρ and R sliders. Show all numerical values.


Widget E – Simulator uzgona: tijelo proizvoljnog oblika
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget demonstrating buoyancy as the net result of pressure forces on ALL surfaces of a submerged body. Draw a rectangle (which the user can morph into a cylinder-like shape using a "roundness" slider from 0=rectangle to 1=circle). Show pressure arrows pointing inward (normal to surface) at many points around the entire perimeter: bottom arrows point UP (large, high pressure), top arrows point DOWN (smaller, lower pressure), side arrows point horizontally (cancel left-right by symmetry). Animate the arrows: bottom ones are longer than top ones — the NET upward force is the buoyancy. Show a running sum panel: ΣF_top (downward) in red, ΣF_bottom (upward) in blue, ΣF_sides (cancel) in grey. Net buoyancy = ΣF_bottom - ΣF_top = ρ·g·V displayed live. Add sliders for: submersion depth, object height and width, density ρ. Add a "Show Archimedes equivalent" toggle that replaces all the arrows with a single upward buoyancy arrow F_L = ρ·g·V and shows a ghost of the displaced fluid volume. Animate the transition between the two views smoothly. This connects curved surface pressure integration directly to Archimedes' principle.


Widget F – Moment ravnoteže zaklopke: kada se otvara?
Prompt:

Create an interactive HTML/JS/CSS widget simulating a curved gate (quarter-circle arc) hinged at its top point, holding back water. The gate can rotate open when the net moment about the hinge exceeds zero. Add sliders for: water level H (0–5 m), gate radius R, gate width b, gate mass m (which creates a closing gravitational moment), hinge position. Draw the gate as a quarter-circle arc, hinged at top. Show pressure arrows along the gate surface, normal to the curve, all pointing into the gate. Decompose into F_H (horizontal, shown with its line of action and moment arm to hinge) and F_V (vertical, shown with its line of action and moment arm to hinge). Show gravity force G on gate with its moment arm. Display a live moment balance bar chart: M_water_opens (blue bar) vs M_gravity_closes (red bar). When M_water > M_gravity, animate the gate swinging open with a smooth rotation animation. Add a critical water level indicator that auto-calculates and marks the exact H at which the gate opens. Students can drag the water level up and watch the moment bars and gate respond in real time.
